About Us

In the history of CINEC Campus Malabe, a ZeroPlastic Unit has been developed with the assistance of youth volunteers, who represent Sri Lanka's Zero Plastic National Movement. The ZeroPlastic Unit is dedicated to adapting efforts to minimize single-use plastics, boost recycling, and encourage the implementation of sustainable practices. The unit, on the other hand, wants to raise awareness and educate students and staff on the environmental effects of plastic use. It will collaborate with local communities, companies, and other environmental groups to increase the initiative's impact. It also focuses on finding alternate solutions to the plastic scourge. Since its inception in 2023, the ZeroPlastic Unit of CINEC has completed several projects including the Clean-Up Project at Chandrika Kumaratunga Mawatha and a Climate Action project within the university premises.

Objectives

Build social behaviour change by adapting to 3R.

Get people used to sustainable alternatives.

Expand and empower the ZeroPlastic network.

The movement consists of 9000+ dedicated volunteers engaged in raising awareness across universities, schools and other districts around the country.
